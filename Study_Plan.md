Built from your notes. Priority order stays fixed every day: **AI first, COBOL second, Chinese third** — but every day touches all three, as you wanted.

## Daily structure (every day)

| Block | Priority | Rough share of session | What it covers |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI | 1 | ~60% | Current focus sub-topic, learn → implement → fix → repeat |
| COBOL | 2 | ~25% | Rotating material (video / book / PDF / thesis) |
| Chinese | 3 | ~15% | Textbook/workbook or Gemini conversation, **plus** daily word-list drill |

- **AI "daily ops" skim** — a quick pass over your AI news sites / PDFs (NotebookLM) for current awareness.
- **Chinese word-list drill** — deliberate practice of the words you've collected, ~5–10 minutes. This is the one thing you called out as needing to be deliberate, so it shouldn't get skipped even on light days.

## Weekly rotation

Apply this template to whichever AI sub-topic is currently active. The "AI action" column maps to your stated workflow: **learn → implement → fix → repeat**, then **video + 1 implementation** once a topic clicks.

| Day | AI action (current sub-topic) | COBOL material | Chinese activity |

| Mon | **Learn**: Stanford lecture / PDF via NotebookLM | YouTube tutorial | Textbook lesson |
